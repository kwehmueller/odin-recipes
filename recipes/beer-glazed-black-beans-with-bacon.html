Beer Glazed Black Beans with Bacon

Description

One of the notable modifications to Mark Bittman's recipe in How to Cook Everything, this one has been completely devoured at every single cookout I've attended for the better part of a decade.

>

This is an amazing, flavorful dish to have in the back of your mind, especially on a budget. It's easy to put together, and each ingredident aside from your choice of beer and the optional bacon can reach your cabinet for under a dollar.

Ingredients

  • Yellow onion
  • Garlic
  • Beer
  • Bacon
  • Black beans
  • Chili powder
  • Honey
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Place 6-8 oz. of bacon on a cool, non-stick skillet.Heat to medium-high. Cook until until finished on one side--about 5 minutes--flip, and then cook most of the way through on the second side. Transfer to a cutting board, roughly chop, and then set aside. Reserve the bacon grease in the skillet for the next step.
  2. Chop or dice the onion and add to the hot skillet. Cook until soft, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add 1 tbsp. of minced garlic and cook for about a minute.
  4. Add 8 oz. of beer, your choice. Lagers and wheat beers are pretty mellow, but still shine through. Darker beers will make the dish very rich.
  5. Add 3 cups (24 oz.) of black beans, drained but still moist. Stir in with the beer.
  6. Add 1 tbsp. of chili powder, 1 tbsp. of honey, and a healthy sprinkle of salt and pepper. Give the mix a good stir, and then let the heat bring it to a steady bubble.
  7. Cook for about 10 minutes, and then add the partially cooked bacon.Then, cook for another 5 minutes, making sure the bacon is cooked all the way through.
  8. When the mixture is reduced, taste and adjust the seasoning to your preference.
  9. Serve hot, and enjoy with the rest of the beer you used for the recipe.
