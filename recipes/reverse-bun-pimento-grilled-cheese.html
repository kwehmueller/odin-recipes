Reverse-Bun Pimento Grilled Cheese

Description

This recipe is, in its first part, the Blue Ribbon Pimento Cheese that opens up The Southern Foodways Alliance Community Cookbook. Credit goes to Nan Davis and her aunt Ella, as well as book editors, Sara Roahen and John T. Edge. I won't include Lella's mayonnaise recipe; for that, I encourage you to buy the book. Without it, in Lella's words, "you might as well not bother."

The second part is a little homegrown gluttony. No credit required.

Ingredients

Part 1: the cheese

  • Cheddar cheese
  • Pimentos
  • Lella's mayonnaise
  • Onion powder
  • Cayenne pepper
  • Worcester suace
  • Sugar

Part 2: the sandwiches

  • Burger buns
  • Arugla
  • Tomato
  • Ham
  • Pickles
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Grate 5 cups of cheddar cheese and load into a food processor. Drain and add a 4 oz. jar of whole pimentos, and then pulse until they are finely chopped.
  2. Add ½ cup of Lella's mayonnaise into the processor and then pulse to combine.
  3. Add your dry ingredients--¼ tsp of onion powder, an equal amount of cayenne pepper, and a pinch of sugar--as well as ¼ tsp of Worcester sauce, and then pulse the mixture until it's a smooth, solid colored consistency.
  4. If your mixture is a little too dry, add up to another ½ cup of Lella's mayonnaise and pulse until smooth.
  5. Grab your burger buns, preferably a brioche, pretzel, or sourdough.Spread a little butter on the inside of the buns, and then flip.On the outside of the buns, spread a healthy layer of the pimento cheese.
  6. Assemble the sandwich layer by layer. I like to start with pickles, then arugla, ham, tomato and then top with the second bun, buttered side (the inside of the bun) facing out.
  7. Heat a pan or a panini press to medium low, hot enough to get the cheese melted but not burn the buns.
  8. Toast until the cheese is melted and the bun is a nice golden brown.
  9. Serve with chips, and a little grace for yourself.
